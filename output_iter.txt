Most of the objections to the funding allocations focused on two initiatives: Sustainable Food Production and the Health and Wellness Program.  

Several voters felt that the amount allocated for the Sustainable Food Production initiative was too low given their backgrounds and interests in sustainable and local food production, cooking and nutrition. They believed their skills could contribute greatly to this project if more resources were provided.    

Many voters also expressed that the Health and Wellness Program funding was disproportionately small compared to their stated support for holistic health initiatives and interest in promoting healthy lifestyles. They wanted to see a higher funding amount to enable more community health and fitness programs.

A few objections also mentioned that the "Art and Culture Enrichment" allocation was too small for voters with artistic backgrounds who wanted to see more support for projects fostering creativity and cultural expression.

In summary, decision makers should evaluate increasing the funding for the Sustainable Food Production, Health and Wellness Program and possibly Art and Culture Enrichment initiatives to address the key concerns raised by these voters.
